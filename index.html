 The horrified Costco worker

At Costco my three year-old daughter was chatting up a woman in charge of a samples booth, breaking it down for her how dolls work.

When they were done we continued shopping, pausing at intervals to sample the wares, and completely forgot about the samples booth lady... or so I thought. It turns out all this time a thought had been turning over in that 3 year-old mind.

After we had made the rounds and were heading toward the front of the store, we approached the booth again. My kid marched confidently ahead, stopped in front of booth and announced her conclusion to the woman: "I don't know if you're a boy or a girl."

Before the woman had time to react, I said "Yeah, I think it's in aisle 7, gimme a second!" to my new imaginary friend, grabbed my kid's hand and disappeared around the corner. And that's what kids'll do for you.

 The unconscious samples guy

I begin with a haiku:

Costco samples man
Fallen... EMTs assist
Hungry shoppers torn

True Story. The guy was old, but he was attended to, and everyone gathered around looked very concerned (or opportunistic, it's hard to tell those apart). Anyway, you might be wondering how it all ended. Well, let's just say I got a few extra pigs-in-a-blanket that day. It's good when people come together.

 The fast car

Some people get mad when a fast driver passes them. Not me. I understand that the entire point of driving is for others to safely and quickly get out of my way. And I firmly believe that the more people knew this, the happier we'd all be. So when some dude zips by me on the freeway, I just smile and nod, 'cause that guy gets it.

I will never cease to be amazed at how fearless, raw and uninhibited children can be when extracting a wedgie in Costco.
